Why Are We Learning This?

Artificial intelligence (AI) tools create text and pictures by learning from online data. If that data includes stereotypes or missing voices, AI might unintentionally repeat them. This activity helps you see, question, and redesign unfair AI results so that technology better represents everyone.

1. Spot the Bias

Use Canva Text-to-Image to explore how AI represents people. If your teacher allows, compare it with Bing Image Creator.

  1. Enter one or more of these prompts and generate at least two images for each:
    • "a scientist working in a laboratory"
    • "a teacher in a classroom"
    • "a leader giving a speech"
    • "a student studying at home"
    • "a nurse helping a patient"
  2. Look closely at the results and record your observations:
    • Who appears most often, and who is missing?
    • Do the images show stereotypes about gender, culture or roles?
    • What would a fairer image include?
Remember: AI doesn't think - it predicts what a "typical" image might look like using human data. If those patterns are biased, the results can be too.

2. Why Does Bias Happen?

AI bias can appear for several reasons:

  • Biased data: Most training images come from one culture or group.
  • Design choices: Algorithms often value speed or popularity instead of fairness.
  • Human influence: Prompts and feedback can reinforce bias.

Knowing this helps you think critically about how technology shapes what you see online.

3. Redesign for Fairness

Create a digital poster in Canva showing how you would make AI more inclusive.

  1. Add one biased AI image you found.
  2. Explain in a short paragraph what kind of bias it shows.
  3. Redesign or describe a fairer version that includes more diversity.

You may use AI tools for ideas or proofreading, but you remain the author. Add a line at the bottom: "AI assistance used for idea generation only."

4. Reflect and Share

Share your poster in the class AI Fairness Gallery. As you review classmates' work, discuss:

  • How did their redesign make the image more inclusive?
  • What responsibilities do humans have when using AI?
Write a short reflection (about 100 words):
"One way I can help make AI fairer is..."

Support and Extension

  • Need help? Use the class vocabulary cards to clarify new terms.
  • Need a challenge? Compare Canva Text-to-Image with Bing Image Creator and explain which produces fairer results and why.
  • EAL/D tip: You may translate key words to build understanding, but express your final work in English.
