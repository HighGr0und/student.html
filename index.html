Can AI Be Fair?

Why Are We Learning This?

Artificial intelligence (AI) tools create text and pictures by learning from large collections of online data. If that data includes stereotypes or missing voices, AI may unintentionally repeat those patterns. This activity helps you see, question and redesign unfair AI results so that technology better represents everyone.

1. Spot the Bias

Use Canva Text-to-Image to explore how AI represents people. If your teacher allows, you may also compare it with Bing Image Creator.

Follow these steps:

  1. Enter one or more of these prompts and generate at least two images for each:
    • "a scientist working in a laboratory"
    • "a teacher in a classroom"
    • "a leader giving a speech"
    • "a student studying at home"
    • "a nurse helping a patient"
  2. Look carefully at the results and record your observations:
    • Who appears most often, and who is missing?
    • Do the images show stereotypes about gender, culture or jobs?
    • What would a fairer image include?
Remember: AI does not think. It predicts what a "typical" image might look like based on human data. If those patterns are biased, the results can be too.

2. Why Does Bias Happen?

AI bias can appear for several reasons:

  • Biased data: Most training images come from only one group or culture.
  • Design choices: Algorithms might value popularity or speed instead of fairness.
  • Human influence: Prompts and user feedback can add new bias.

Understanding these reasons helps you think critically about how technology shapes what you see online.

3. Redesign for Fairness

Create a digital poster in Canva showing how you would make AI more inclusive.

  1. Add one biased AI image you found.
  2. Explain in a short paragraph what kind of bias it shows.
  3. Redesign or describe a fairer version that includes more diversity.

You may use AI tools for idea generation or proofreading, but you remain the author. Add a note at the bottom of your work: "AI assistance used for idea generation only."

4. Reflect and Share

Share your poster in the class AI Fairness Gallery. As you view classmates' work, discuss:

  • How did their redesign make the image more inclusive?
  • What responsibilities do humans have when using AI?
Write a short reflection (about 100 words):
"One way I can help make AI fairer is..."

Support and Extension

  • Need help? Use the class vocabulary cards.
  • Need a challenge? Compare Canva Text-to-Image with Bing Image Creator and explain which produces fairer results.
  • EAL/D tip: Use translation tools to check key words, but write your final reflection in English.
